Overview

  • VietAbroader Hanoi (VA HN) was founded and sustained by VietAbroader organization in 2008. Despite demonstrating a diverse array of origins, characteristics, and aspirations, VietAbroader Hanoi as a whole is wholeheartedly dedicated to “creating values for students and to students”. This prefixed commitment is the driving force behind our ample successful stories.
  • Since officially set in motion, VietAbroader Hanoi had held 4 annual events, 3 impromptu affairs and co-chaired numerous professional seminars and workshops. Some of VA HN’s outstanding operations in the previous working year include: organizing the entrepreneurial innovation tournament- Teen Entrepreneur, executing Vocational orientation seminars - Polaris and iLead Challenge.

Problem & Motivation

    Entering the 13th year in operation, after years of imposing the make-shift model, lacking clear organizational vision, defined implementing strategies, and in-depth expertise, VA HN grappled with problematic financial challenges and inconsistent brandings that lead to insignificant community values. As VA HN 13th’s president, I must decide between 1. holistically restructuring VA HN’s model to revitalize the organization or 2. continuing to let VA HN dwindle.

Changes in strategy

    In retrospect, I was convinced the most prominent obstacle that prevented VA HN from creating more communal values was finance management. As one who used to retail takeaways on the Walking street to raise funds for my first event, I am fully aware of the key role finance plays in operating and sustaining an organization.

    Realizing our major source of financial support was sponsors with a student targeted demographics, instead of displaying everything we had to offer, I embarked on asking myself 2 questions:

    1. What values do businesses need?

    2. What values can we transpire?

    Branding marketing: Regulating a 30k follow account was not a decisive advantage as cross-promotion post-interaction only accounts for 5% of viewers (1k-2k reach).

    Sales: Out of reach for a student-operated organization.
